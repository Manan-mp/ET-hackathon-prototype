Report Cyber Fraud

If you or someone you know has been targeted by a digital arrest scam, report immediately through these official channels:

1

Call the Cyber Crime Helpline

Dial 1930 (National Cyber Crime Helpline). Available 24/7. Report the incident with as many details as possible.

2

File an Online Complaint

Visit cybercrime.gov.in and file a complaint under "Financial Fraud". Keep your reference number for tracking.

3

Visit the Nearest Police Station

File an FIR at your local police station. Carry screenshots of calls, messages, and any transaction receipts.

4

Contact Your Bank Immediately

If you've transferred money, call your bank's fraud helpline immediately to attempt a reversal. Time is critical — the sooner you report, the higher the chance of recovery.

Remember: No government agency — CBI, ED, Police, RBI, TRAI, or Customs — will ever arrest you over a phone/video call, ask you to transfer money for "verification", or demand you stay on a video call. If someone does this, it is a scam. Hang up immediately.
